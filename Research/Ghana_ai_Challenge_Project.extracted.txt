1. Problem Statement &amp; Objectives
Ghana's agricultural sector employs over 40% of the population, yet smallholder farmers continue to suffer severe crop losses due to pests and diseases. Cashew, cassava, maize, and tomato — four of Ghana's most cultivated crops — are particularly vulnerable to a wide range of infestations including fall armyworm, mosaic virus, bacterial blight, and leaf blight. Early detection is critical, but most farmers lack access to trained agronomists, and the knowledge gap between technology and rural practitioners remains wide.

This project aims to:
Build an AI-powered mobile application that enables farmers to detect crop pests and diseases in real time using their smartphone cameras
Develop a low-cost embedded scouting device for farmers without smartphones, using a Raspberry Pi with a camera module housed in a 3D-printed casing
Deploy both solutions offline, ensuring usability in rural areas with limited internet connectivity
Provide actionable treatment recommendations immediately following a diagnosis

3. Methodology &amp; AI/ML Techniques
3.1 Shared AI Model
Both deliverables (mobile app and embedded device) share a single trained model, ensuring development efficiency and consistent performance across deployment platforms.
Architecture: MobileNetV2 or EfficientNet-Lite (selected after benchmarking for accuracy vs. inference speed trade-off)
Framework: TensorFlow / Keras for training; TensorFlow Lite for on-device deployment
Training: Transfer learning from ImageNet pretrained weights, fine-tuned on the Ghana crop dataset
Data augmentation: Rotation, flipping, brightness/contrast variation to improve model robustness
Output: 22-class softmax classifier covering all crop-pest/disease combinations

3.2 Project 1 — Mobile Diagnosis App
Platform: C# .Net Maui (cross-platform Android/iOS)
Model deployed as a .tflite file bundled within the app — no internet required
Farmer captures or uploads a leaf photo; model runs inference on-device in under 2 seconds
Results screen shows: detected disease/pest name, confidence score, and a simple treatment recommendation
Supports Twi and English UI labels for local accessibility

3.3 Project 2 — Embedded Scouting Device
Hardware: Raspberry Pi Zero 2W + OV5647 camera module
Enclosure: Custom 3D-printed casing designed in CAD (Fusion 360), optimised for field use
Output: 2-inch LCD display showing disease name + a colour-coded severity LED indicator
Power: Rechargeable Li-Po battery pack; estimated 6-hour field runtime
Firmware: Python-based inference pipeline using TFLite Runtime
No smartphone or internet connectivity required — fully standalone

5. Potential Impact &amp; Relevance
Ghana loses an estimated 30–40% of annual crop yields to preventable pest and disease outbreaks. Early detection, which currently depends on expensive agronomist visits, is out of reach for most smallholder farmers. This system directly addresses that gap by placing diagnostic capability in the hands of farmers at zero recurring cost.

The mobile app targets the estimated 14 million smartphone users in Ghana, requiring no data connection in the field
The embedded device extends reach to farmers without smartphones — particularly in Northern and rural Ashanti regions
Both tools align with Ghana's MoFA digitisation agenda and the National AI Strategy's focus on agriculture
A single trained model serving two form factors demonstrates clear scalability and cost-efficiency
Potential for integration with the Esoko and AgroCenta platforms already serving Ghanaian smallholder farmers
